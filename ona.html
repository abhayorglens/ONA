Expert Insights: Cognitive Networks and Organizational Connectivity

Dr. Martin Kilduff

Dr. Kilduff is known for his work on Social Networks and Organizational Behavior. His research delves into the psychological foundations of networks, emphasizing how individual cognition affects social network structures.

Dr. Starling Hunter

Dr. Hunter has contributed significantly to Semantic Networks and Organizational Communication. His research explores how communication patterns within organizations reflect underlying power structures.

Personality, Inclusion, and Social Capital in Organizational Networks

Personality & Social Capital - Big 5 Applications to Networks

Professor Prasad Balkundi's work explores how leaders' personalities shape their social networks, influencing their ability to build alliances and lead effectively. His research highlights how traits like openness and extroversion impact interactions within networks, driving organizational success.

Inclusion & Social Capital in Organizations

Dr. Vernet's research focuses on Network Dynamics and Innovation. He studies how networks evolve over time and how this evolution impacts knowledge diffusion and innovation within organizations, and its impact on minorities & Inclusion.

Trust and Strategic Applications in Organizational Network Analysis

Trust in Organization Social Networks

Dr. Jha's research examines Interpersonal Networks and Organizational Resilience. She has focused on how social capital within organizations helps build resilience during crises.

Her work highlights the importance of trust in fostering strong interpersonal networks, which in turn contribute to an organization's ability to withstand and adapt to challenging situations.

Other Strategic Applications of ONA

Santhosh Babu, founder of OD Alternatives, has over two decades of experience as a coach, leadership facilitator, and culture expert, dedicated to helping organizations grow and thrive.

Stage 2: Important Steps for Hands-On ONA Study

Survey initiation and Data collection

ONA study is an important part of the course, and needs to be initiated in November to ensure timely completion and analysis. Please ensure you get all the necessary Organizational approvals, and data of the participants before the course begins. Nov 20th - Jan 10th

1. Finalize Participants and Seek Organizational Approval ( By Nov 10th)

Draft a formal communication to seek approval from the organization - Sample Email Draft

Ensure that participants are briefed about the process and the importance of ONA in improving collaboration & communication - Sample Email Draft.

2. Choose Your Tools ( By Nov 20th)

We recommend using the OrgLens tool, as it offers seamless integration for conducting Organizational Network Analysis & will be available free of cost.

If you prefer to use open-source tools like Gephi, NodeXL, or NetMiner, please consult with the OrgLens team for guidance.

3. Survey Design ( By Nov 20th)

The survey focuses on 5 key questions about participants' interactions with their colleagues for different reasons.

With the OrgLens tool, the survey design & questionnaire are already taken care of; however, if you're using open- source tools, you'll need to ensure the survey follows a similar structure

4. Data Collection ( Nov 20th - Dec 20th)

Provide participants with clear instructions and a timeline for completing the survey

Ensure the data collection process is straightforward, with reminders to participants about the importance of submitting their responses on time

Remember, the OrgLens team is here to support you throughout this process, ensuring you gain valuable hands-on experience in Organizational Network Analysis.

Stage 3: Data Analysis and Generation of Relevant Organisational Insights

Learn from your & your Peers organizational insights - Presentations from Jan 10th to Jan 30th

1

Information Collected and Data Analysis

Summarize the types of data collected during the ONA study. Highlight key findings and metrics that emerged from the analysis.

2

Overlay Organizational Context

Connect the analyzed data to the organizational context, illustrating how external and internal factors (such as organizational goals, culture, and structure) influence the observed trends and behaviors within the network.

3

Create Patterns and Insights for Your Organization

Identify specific patterns and Present insights that can help understand the dynamics within the organization and identify opportunities for improvement.

4

Inputs for Organizational Development Based on Insights

Provide actionable recommendations for organizational development based on the insights generated.

Stage 3:

You will analyse the ONA data collected and choose a topic to dive deeper, basis what's most relevant to your organisation.

In the upcoming pages, we've provided some sample topics for you to choose from.

Topics you can delve deeper into (1/3)

Networks & Performance

Explore the correlation between network parameters (e.g., average connectivity, diversity of connections, reciprocity) and individual performance.

Tenure & Trust

Analyze how tenure influences trust generation within the organization.

Decision-Making & Networks

Investigate whether employees holding critical network positions have a greater influence on decision- making within the organization.

Attrition & Networks

Explore how networks can potentially indicate an employee's attrition risk.

Topics you can delve deeper into (2/3)

Personal Networks & Inclusion

Examine how stronger personal networks may lead to higher inclusion levels among employees, and how these networks vary by gender or tenure.

Gender & Trust

Study the impact of gender on trust-building within organizational networks.

Formal Roles & Networks

Assess whether the formal roles of employees (manager, individual contributor, leader) affect their organizational networks.

Gender & Innovation Networks

Analyze the connection between gender and innovation levels within the organization.

Topics you can delve deeper into (3/3)

Team Structure & Individual Performance

Investigate how a team's informal structure impacts individual performance.

Managerial Style & Individual Performance

Examine the correlation between traditional managerial styles and the informal networks created within teams, and their influence on employee performance.

Psychometric Evaluations & Informal Roles

Evaluate whether individual styles, as determined by psychometric tests, correlate with the informal roles employees play within the organization.

Tenure & Innovation Networks

Investigate the correlation between employee tenure and innovation within the organization.

These insights are meant to spark your creativity. Craft projects that align perfectly with your organizational context as well your own unique perspective and goals
